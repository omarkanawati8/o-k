Omar Kanawati

Transformations in contemporary calligraphy

About

Omar Kanawati's practice investigates the materiality of writing through innovative use of corrosive fluids and metal alloys as substitutes for conventional ink and paper.

In 2016, he graduated from the Academy of Fine Arts and Design in Bratislava with a master's degree in Paper Restoration and Conservation. His research paper focused on the conservation and restoration of Japanese Prints Ukiyo-e artworks. Subsequently, in 2020, Omar obtained a second Master of Arts degree in Chinese Painting and Calligraphy from the China Academy of Art in Hangzhou. Currently pursuing PhD studies in the department of Chinese calligraphy at China Academy of Art.

During his studies and following the graduation, Omar has actively participated in major exhibitions, including the International Exhibition of Modern Calligraphy "Writing Non-Writing"(2019), the "Biennial Exhibition of Lanting Calligraphy Association"(2012) and the “Exhibition-in-suitcases” at the CAPC Musée d’art Contemporain in Bordeaux(2025).

His research at the master's level delved into the intricacies of Qin dynasty Seal Script and Han dynasty Clerical Script. After graduation, Omar concentrated on the use of mixed media in calligraphy, specifically focusing on experimenting with corrosive fluids and metal alloys as a substitute for ink and paper as the primary medium of calligraphy and building an extensive artistic practice combining the use of these materials and processes with the traditional approaches to Chinese calligraphy.

Performance

"Question Mark Nth" Live Calligraphy Performance

2021, Hangzhou, China
Duration: 45 minutes

A live performance exploring the boundaries between traditional calligraphy and contemporary art practice. Using corrosive fluids on metal plates, the performance transforms the act of writing into a temporal, evolving piece.

"Alchemical Transmutations"

2023, Shanghai International Art Festival
Duration: 90 minutes

An extended performance piece combining live calligraphy with chemical reactions. The artist creates temporary works that evolve throughout the performance, documenting the ephemeral nature of material transformations.

"Silent Characters"

2022, Modern Calligraphy Research Centre, Hangzhou
Duration: 60 minutes

A meditative performance exploring the relationship between breath, movement, and the creation of calligraphic forms. Using specially prepared papers that react to body temperature and humidity.

"Reverse Movement"

2025, TEI Conference, Bordeaux
Duration: 30 minutes

A collaborative performance with Iurii Kuzmin and Raul Masu, exploring tangible interaction through calligraphic gestures. The piece investigates how digital technologies can capture and reinterpret traditional art forms.
