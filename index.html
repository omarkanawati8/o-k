Omar Kanawati

Transformations in contemporary calligraphy

About

Omar Kanawati's practice investigates the materiality of writing through innovative use of corrosive fluids and metal alloys as substitutes for conventional ink and paper.

In 2016, he graduated from the Academy of Fine Arts and Design in Bratislava with a master's degree in Paper Restoration and Conservation. His research paper focused on the conservation and restoration of Japanese Prints Ukiyo-e artworks. Subsequently, in 2020, Omar obtained a second Master of Arts degree in Chinese Painting and Calligraphy from the China Academy of Art in Hangzhou. Currently pursuing PhD studies in the department of Chinese calligraphy at China Academy of Art.

During his studies and following the graduation, Omar have actively participated in major exhibitions, including the International Exhibition of Modern Calligraphy "Writing Non-Writing", the "Biennial Exhibition of Lanting Calligraphy Association" and the “Exhibition-in-suitcases” at the CAPC Musée d’art Contemporian in Bordeaux.

His research at the master's level delved into the intricacies of Qin dynasty Seal Script and Han dynasty Clerical Script. After graduation Omar concentrated on the use of mixed media in calligraphy, specifically focusing on experimenting with corrosive fluids and metal alloys as a substitute for ink and paper as the primary medium of calligraphy and building an extensive artistic practice combining the use of these materials and processes with the traditional approaches to Chinese calligraphy.

In 2020, Omar obtained a second Master of Arts degree in Chinese Painting and Calligraphy from the China Academy of Art in Hangzhou. He is currently pursuing PhD studies in Chinese calligraphy at the same institution.
